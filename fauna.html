FAUNA

Fauna refers to the animal life that is found in a particular region or ecosystem. It encompasses all types of animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, and invertebrates. Fauna plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of ecosystems and providing essential services such as pollination, seed dispersal, and pest control.

The study of fauna is known as zoology, which involves the classification, identification, and understanding of animal species. Zoologists study the characteristics of animals, their behavior patterns, and their interactions with other organisms in the ecosystem. This knowledge is essential for conservation efforts and sustainable management of natural resources.

Conservation of fauna is critical for maintaining biodiversity and ensuring the health of ecosystems. Many animal species are threatened or endangered due to habitat loss, climate change, pollution, and overexploitation. Conservation efforts include habitat restoration, captive breeding programs, and legal protections to prevent the hunting and trade of endangered animal species.
